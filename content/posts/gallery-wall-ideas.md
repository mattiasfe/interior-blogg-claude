There are objects that stop a room from feeling finished, and there are objects that make a room feel like it has always been exactly right. The antique cabinet in this image is the second kind. Its paint — multiple layers of blue, red and yellow, peeling and worn at the corners — tells decades of history. The blue ceramic vase on top holds wildflowers so casually you'd think they were just cut from the garden. The wall behind it is bare and imperfect. The whole thing looks like it has never tried at all, which is exactly why it works.

## Styling vintage furniture with flowers

Fresh flowers change everything about a vintage piece. Where a sculpture or a lamp might feel too deliberate, flowers are alive — they introduce movement, scent and unpredictability. The arrangement here is deliberately loose: cosmos, dahlias and grasses at varying heights, nothing clipped or formal. The taller stems extend well above the vase, which gives the composition height and stops it looking squat.

## Choosing the right vase

The blue ceramic vase here is the right choice for a reason: its colour deepens the blue in the cabinet's paint, creating a tonal connection without being matchy. A vase that shares at least one colour with the furniture it sits on almost always looks considered. The shape — wide base, narrow neck, traditional form — suits the antique aesthetic of the cabinet.

## Embracing chipped and layered paint

The appeal of this cabinet is inseparable from its worn surface. Repainting it — even in the same colours, fresh and even — would destroy what makes it special. If you find a vintage piece with multiple layers of paint showing at the edges, leave it. Polish and wax it if needed, but resist the urge to restore it to a pristine finish.

## Placing vintage pieces against plain walls

A wall with a rough or imperfect plaster texture, as seen here, suits a distressed vintage piece perfectly. A fresh, smooth white wall would make the same cabinet look incongruous — too old for its surroundings. If your walls are very clean and modern, consider a textured paint finish or limewashing a single wall to create a surface the vintage piece can sit comfortably against.

## Interior tips

- **Use wildflowers rather than florist arrangements**: garden flowers, hedgerow clippings and cut herbs have an informality that suits vintage furniture perfectly.
- **Let the background be simple**: the cabinet is the subject, the flowers are the accent. Don't add other objects to the same surface.
- **A small chair or stool beside the cabinet** reinforces the sense of a curated corner rather than a piece standing alone.
- **Sourcing tip**: look for cabinets like this at estate sales, flea markets and online auction platforms. Pieces with unusual paint histories are usually undervalued because buyers lack confidence in how to use them.
- **Mix eras**: a very old piece of furniture in a contemporary room isn't a mismatch — it's a conversation. The contrast between old and new is one of the defining qualities of interesting interior design.
