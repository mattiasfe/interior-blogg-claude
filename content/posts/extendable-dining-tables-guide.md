An extendable dining table is one of the smartest investments you can make for a small home — it adapts to your life instead of dictating it.

## Drop-leaf tables

Drop-leaf tables can shrink down to a narrow console against the wall and expand to seat four to six people when needed.

## Butterfly-leaf tables

A hidden leaf folds out from underneath the tabletop, making these tables quick to extend without storing extra parts.

## Console-to-dining tables

Some tables fold completely flat against the wall and unfold into a full dining table — ideal for studio apartments.

## Round tables with leaves

Round tables feel less bulky in small rooms and can often be extended to oval shapes for extra guests.

## What to look for

- A tabletop material that's easy to clean (laminate, sealed wood, or veneer)
- Sturdy legs that don't wobble when extended
- A size that fits your space when both folded and expanded

With the right extendable table, you'll never have to choose between a functional everyday space and being ready to host.
