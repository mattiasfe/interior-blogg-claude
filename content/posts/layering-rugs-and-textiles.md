This wall organiser is a piece of functional art. A large crochet panel in natural jute, hung from a wooden dowel on leather straps, with pockets in three different materials — more crochet, soft leather and small wicker baskets. Inside those pockets: a leather journal, a spiral notebook, dried lavender and sunflowers, a ball of yarn, a crochet hook, buttons, art brushes and pencils. It is organised chaos made beautiful, and it hangs on a white wall like a tapestry.

## What makes this organiser different

Most desk and wall organisers are plastic, metal or painted wood. They store things efficiently but add nothing to the room. A crochet organiser like this does the opposite: the texture, the natural fibres and the mix of pocket materials turn a functional object into something you'd choose to look at even if it weren't useful.

## The structure: dowel, straps and panel

The organiser hangs from a natural wood dowel — a thick branch or turned wooden rod — suspended by two leather straps from wall hooks. The strap mechanism means the panel hangs flat and can be easily removed and rehung. The crochet panel itself should be stiff enough to hold its shape but loose enough to drape naturally.

## The three pocket types

**Crochet pockets** — the base material, worked in the same jute or natural cotton as the panel. Loose weave allows you to see the contents while keeping them in place.

**Leather pockets** — either purchased or cut from a piece of vegetable-tanned leather. The contrast of stiff, smooth leather against the soft crochet is the visual highlight of the piece.

**Wicker basket pockets** — small rattan or wicker baskets stitched or tied to the panel. These are the deepest pockets, ideal for bulkier items like a dried flower bunch, a ball of yarn or a collection of tools.

## What to put in it

The contents visible here are carefully chosen: dried botanicals (lavender, sunflowers, eucalyptus) in the wicker pockets add colour and scent. Leather journals and notebooks sit in the leather pockets. Art and craft tools — brushes, pencils, a crochet hook — fill the smaller pockets. The mix of practical and decorative is deliberate and very effective.

## Interior tips

- **Hang it in a home office or craft room** where the contents are actually needed — a beautiful organiser that requires you to cross the room to use it defeats the purpose.
- **Refresh the dried flowers seasonally**: swap lavender for eucalyptus in winter, add dried grasses and seed heads in autumn. The organiser becomes a seasonal object.
- **Keep one pocket empty** as a visual rest — a completely filled organiser stops reading as decor and starts reading as storage.
- **Natural fibre on natural fibre looks best**: jute, linen and cotton crochet suits leather and rattan. Synthetic yarns lose the artisanal quality entirely.
- **A wooden dowel with visible grain** is better than a painted or lacquered one — the natural texture ties back to the other materials in the piece.
