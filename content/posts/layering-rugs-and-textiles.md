Layering textiles is one of the simplest ways to make a room feel finished, warm and a little more personal.

## Start with a neutral base rug

A large, neutral rug anchors the room and gives you a calm canvas to layer on top of.

## Add a smaller patterned rug

Place a smaller rug with pattern or texture on top, slightly offset, to define a seating area or add visual interest.

## Mix cushion fabrics

Combine linen, wool and a touch of velvet or boucle for contrast. Aim for a mix of solid colors and one or two patterns.

## Don't forget the throw

A chunky knit or wool throw draped over a sofa arm or chair back adds instant coziness and a sense of being lived-in.

## Layer curtains with sheers

A sheer curtain layered behind a heavier drape softens the light and adds depth to windows.

## Repeat one material across the room

Choose one material — like linen or boucle — and repeat it in two or three places to tie the layers together.

The result is a room that feels collected over time, not styled in a single afternoon.
