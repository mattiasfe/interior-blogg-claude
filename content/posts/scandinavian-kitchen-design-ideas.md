The kitchen is often the heart of the home, and Scandinavian kitchens prove that practical doesn't have to mean boring.

## Open shelving

Swap a few upper cabinets for open shelves to display ceramics, glassware and cookbooks — it keeps the room feeling open and personal.

## Light wood and white

A combination of light wood cabinetry or countertops with white walls and tile creates a bright, airy base that works with any color accent.

## Hidden storage

Behind the simple facades, Scandinavian kitchens are packed with smart storage — pull-out drawers, built-in spice racks and labeled containers keep clutter out of sight.

## Statement lighting

A single pendant light in a natural material like rattan or glass adds character without competing with the rest of the room.

## Greenery on the windowsill

Fresh herbs in simple pots bring life, scent and function to your cooking space.

Small changes — new hardware, open shelves, a fresh coat of paint — can transform your kitchen without a full renovation.
