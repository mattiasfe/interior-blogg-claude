Empty walls can make a home feel unfinished, but good art doesn't have to be expensive. Here are a few budget-friendly approaches.

## Frame fabric or wallpaper scraps

A leftover piece of patterned fabric or wallpaper, framed simply, can look just as intentional as a printed art piece.

## Print your own photos

High-quality prints of your own travel or nature photos, in matching frames, create a cohesive and personal gallery.

## Look for vintage finds

Flea markets and thrift stores often have framed art for a fraction of retail price — look past the frame and focus on the image.

## Use botanical pages

Pages from an old botanical or nature book, framed individually or in a small grid, add a vintage, collected feel.

## Try oversized single prints

One large, simple print often has more impact — and can be cheaper per square meter — than several small ones.

## Don't underestimate mirrors

A well-placed mirror fills wall space, reflects light, and adds visual interest without needing to be "art" at all.

A little creativity goes a long way toward walls that feel curated rather than empty.
